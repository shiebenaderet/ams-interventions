About Tier 2 Interventions

Tier 2 interventions are targeted supports provided to students who need additional help beyond universal Tier 1 instruction. These interventions are typically delivered to small groups of students (3-6) who share similar needs, or individually when appropriate.

Key Principle: Tier 2 is IN ADDITION TO Tier 1, not instead of. Students receive high-quality core instruction PLUS targeted support.

Characteristics of Quality Tier 2

  • Targeted to specific skill gaps or needs
  • Delivered in small groups (3-6 students) or individually
  • More frequent and intensive than Tier 1
  • Progress monitored regularly (weekly or bi-weekly)
  • Time-limited with clear goals (6-8 weeks typically)
  • Supplemental to, not replacing, core instruction

When to Use Tier 2

Consider Tier 2 interventions when:

  • Student is not responding to high-quality Tier 1 instruction
  • Consistent pattern of difficulty shown through data
  • Specific skill gaps identified through assessment
  • Student needs more practice, reteaching, or support than Tier 1 provides

Implementation Tips for Tier 2

Use Data to Group

Form groups based on specific skill needs identified through assessment, not just general "low" performance. Students in a group should have similar skill gaps.

Keep Groups Small

3-6 students is ideal. Smaller groups allow for more interaction, immediate feedback, and targeted instruction.

Monitor Progress Weekly

Tier 2 requires more frequent progress monitoring than Tier 1. Check progress weekly to ensure intervention is working.

Set Clear Exit Criteria

Know what mastery looks like. When students meet the goal, they exit Tier 2 and return to Tier 1 only.

Need More Intensive Support?

If students are not responding to Tier 2 interventions after 6-8 weeks of consistent implementation, consider:

  • Reviewing implementation fidelity (is the intervention being done as designed?)
  • Adjusting the intervention or trying a different approach
  • Increasing intensity (more time, smaller group, more frequent)
  • Moving to Tier 3 intensive interventions
  • Referring for special education evaluation if appropriate
