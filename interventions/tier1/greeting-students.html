What It Is

Teacher stands at doorway and personally greets each student as they enter the classroom.

What Quality Implementation Looks Like

‚úÖ In the Classroom (What TO Do):

  • Teacher is at door before the bell rings (not arriving same time as students)
  • Eye contact and use of student names
  • Brief, positive interaction (not instructional)
  • Takes attendance mentally as students enter
  • Tone is warm and welcoming
  • Can identify students who need extra support that day

‚ùå Red Flags (What NOT to Do):

  • Teacher at desk while students enter
  • Generic "come in" without eye contact
  • Greeting while looking at phone/computer
  • Used as time to give instructions/corrections
  • Inconsistent (only some days)

Implementation Steps

Week 1: Establish the Routine

Day 1:

  • Stand at door
  • Make eye contact
  • Say each student's name: "Good morning, [Name]"
  • Smile
  • That's it. Keep it simple.

Day 2-5:

  • Continue same greeting
  • Goal: Learn all names by end of week
  • Use seating chart photo if needed

Week 2: Add Personalization

Now that routine is established, add brief personal comments:

  • "How was your soccer practice?"
  • "Did you finish that book?"
  • "I heard you helped in Ms. Garcia's class - thank you!"
Tips:
  • Keep it brief (10 seconds max)
  • Stay positive (don't use this time for corrections)
  • Notice new things (haircut, shoes, mood)

Week 3+: Use for Assessment

As you greet students, you're also:

  • Taking attendance mentally
  • Noticing who seems off (tired, upset, withdrawn)
  • Identifying who needs check-in during class
  • Building data on each student's patterns
Example: You notice Sarah, usually cheerful, barely makes eye contact and mumbles "hi." Mental note: Check in with Sarah today During independent work, quietly ask: "I noticed you seemed down this morning. Everything okay?"

Protocol 3: 2x10 Focused Greeting

For students needing Tier 2 support:

  • Pick 1-2 students per week
  • Greet them FIRST every day
  • Spend extra 30 seconds: "Tell me one good thing from today"
  • Do this for 10 consecutive school days
  • Research shows this builds relationship and improves behavior

Logistics

Q: What if students arrive at different times?

A: Stand at door during the "rush" (most students). When late students arrive, pause and greet them too.

Q: What about passing time between periods?

A: Say goodbye as they leave, greet next class as they arrive. This maximizes doorway time.

Q: What if I need to deal with tardy students or last-minute issues?

A: Post bellwork on board. Students know to enter, do bellwork. You greet, then handle logistics.

Q: What if I teach multiple sections back-to-back?

A: Stand at door during passing period. Students from Period 1 exit, students from Period 2 enter - you're greeting both.
