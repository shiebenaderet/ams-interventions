About Tier 1 Interventions

Tier 1 is the foundation of MTSS and includes universal supports provided to all students in the general education classroom. These interventions should be implemented consistently across all classrooms and are designed to meet the needs of approximately 80% of students.

Key Principle: Tier 1 interventions are for EVERYONE, not just struggling students. When implemented with fidelity, they prevent many students from needing more intensive support.

Characteristics of Quality Tier 1

  • Evidence-based instructional strategies
  • Clear learning objectives and success criteria
  • Differentiation within whole-class instruction
  • Regular formative assessment and feedback
  • Proactive classroom management
  • Positive school climate and relationships

Implementation Tips for Tier 1

Start Small

Choose 2-3 interventions to implement well rather than trying everything at once. Build automaticity before adding more.

Collaborate

Work with your department to implement interventions consistently across classrooms. Students benefit from seeing the same strategies in multiple classes.

Monitor Impact

Use progress monitoring data to determine which Tier 1 interventions are working and which students need Tier 2 support.

Need More Support?

If students are not responding to Tier 1 interventions after consistent implementation (6-8 weeks), consider:

  • Moving to Tier 2 targeted interventions
  • Consulting with your grade-level team or department
  • Reviewing student data to identify specific skill gaps
  • Contacting the counseling team for additional support
