About Tier 3 Interventions

Tier 3 interventions are the most intensive supports in the MTSS framework. They are highly individualized, data-driven, and delivered to students whose needs are not being met through Tier 1 and Tier 2 alone. These interventions often involve multiple adults working collaboratively to support the student.

Critical Principle: Tier 3 is IN ADDITION TO Tiers 1 and 2. Students at Tier 3 still receive high-quality core instruction (Tier 1) and may receive targeted small group support (Tier 2) PLUS intensive individualized intervention.

Characteristics of Quality Tier 3

  • Highly individualized to student's specific needs
  • Intensive (daily or multiple times per week, 30+ minutes)
  • Involves team collaboration (teachers, specialists, counselors, family)
  • Progress monitored very frequently (weekly or more often)
  • Long-term support that may last months or years
  • May include specialized instruction or services
  • Often includes formal plans (IEP, 504, BIP)

When to Use Tier 3

Consider Tier 3 interventions when:

  • Student has not responded to well-implemented Tier 1 and Tier 2 interventions
  • Significant and persistent skill deficits exist
  • Student has a documented disability (IEP or 504)
  • Complex needs require coordination across multiple adults/services
  • Safety concerns require immediate intensive support
Important: Tier 3 supports should be developed by a team, not an individual teacher working alone. Use the Student Support Team process to coordinate intensive interventions.

The Student Support Team Process

Step 1: Refer

Any teacher or staff member can refer a student to the Student Support Team when Tier 1 and 2 interventions are not sufficient.

Step 2: Gather Data

Team collects information from all teachers, specialists, family, and the student. Review academic data, behavior data, attendance, etc.

Step 3: Meet

Team meeting (30-60 min) to analyze data, identify root causes, and develop coordinated Tier 3 intervention plan.

Step 4: Implement

All team members implement their part of the plan consistently. Assign clear roles and responsibilities.

Step 5: Monitor

Progress monitored weekly or more frequently. Team communicates regularly about student progress.

Step 6: Adjust

Team meets every 2-4 weeks to review data and adjust plan as needed. Continue until student is successful or additional evaluation needed.

Legal Considerations

Important: Many Tier 3 interventions have legal requirements:
  • IEPs and 504 Plans: Must be followed exactly as written. Accommodations are legally mandated.
  • Behavior Intervention Plans: Required by law if behavior impedes student's or others' learning.
  • Evaluation Timelines: If considering special education evaluation, specific timelines apply.
  • Parent Rights: Parents must be included in Tier 3 planning and have specific legal rights.

When in doubt, consult with your special education team, counselor, or administrator.

Remember

You are not alone. Tier 3 supports are designed to be collaborative. No single teacher is expected to provide all supports for a student with intensive needs.

Ask for help. If you have a student who needs Tier 3 support, reach out to your Student Support Team, counselor, or case manager.

Document everything. Keep records of interventions tried, student progress, and team communications. This data is essential for making good decisions.
